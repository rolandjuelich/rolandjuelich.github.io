I'm a Hamburg based senior software developer, coach and aspiring software crafter. I constantly strive for higher quality of software solutions that make users happy. Let's start scrolling and learn more about me.

About Me

I'm really passionate about agile software development with a strong dedication to eXtreme Programming. Therefore I'm not only interested in providing value for customers. Moreover I'm interested in keeping this value by continuously deliviering sustainable solutions. Therefore I appreciate the benefits of agile technical practices like Continuous Delivery, Test Driven Development or Pair Programming.

Currently I'm very interested in all topics around distilling domain knowledge and deriving easy testable and maintainable models from it. Therefore concepts like hexagonal architecture as well as knowledge crunching techniques like "Event Storming" are of special interest for me.

Work

SIGNAL IDUNA

Senior Code Crafter + Testing Advisor• November 2019 - Present

Since January 2021 I'm officially in the role of a senior developer in the new "Test Management Squad". Together with a crew of experienced testers, developers and business analysts I work to bring the spirit of a "Quality Mindset" to everyone in the company involved with (agile) software development. Most of the time I help teams with testable designs, test automation, architecture, testing and agility in form of workshops, trainings, facilitation and coaching.

Originally I've been hired to help creating a professional software development team from a group of highly motivated, mostly young, people. The goal was to make them ready to build and run services in any of the companies business domains. One important aspect was focussing on quality because it hardly appeared on the agenda before. Therefore I helped the team applying design techniques like Test Driven Development, principles of Clean Code and patterns from Domain Driven Design. At the end of my first year everybody was assigned to a new squad as a result of a company wide "Agile Transition".

Technologies: Java, Dropwizard, REST, Kafka, MongoDB, Docker, Openshift, JUnit, Cucumber, Serenity BDD, Git, Gradle, Jenkins.

KUEHNE + NAGEL

Senior Software Engineer • January 2015 - October 2019

Most of my time there I've been assigned to the development of the eCommerce platform "KN FreightNet" in the role of an "Agile Test Engineer". However the job had almost nothing to do with "Testing" in a sense of traditional manual testing. Instead I was in charge of supporting the teams to ensure quality as integral part of the development process. This involved establishing practices like BDD, Outside-In TDD, Mob Programming and Explorative Testing. Moreover I learned much about Domain Driven Design and Hexagonal Architecture. Furthermore it allowed me to gain experience on working with multiple distributed teams on several microservices each deployed through its own continuous delivery pipeline and process. After all it sharpened my understanding of what software quality is all about and how teams can achieve it.

Last but not least the technical stack covered pretty much of Java, Spring Boot, REST, SOAP, AMQP (RabbitMQ) up to Cucumber, JBehave, Serenity BDD, Git, Gradle and IntelliJ.

Skills

I believe that quality is important in order to keep a constant pace during development. Although I've never heard a clear definition by now I'd definitely state it not cannot be ensured late as a phase of the developement process.

However if quality is important for the software you build you need certain skills to provide it in every single step of your process. Professional developers typically have a strong desire to aquire necessary skills which go far beyond learning up-to-date technologies. It's rather about aquiring a certain mindset which takes more time by orders of a magnitude. I really enjoy Coding Dojos as an interactive and collaborative learning format for sharing these skills and to train communicational and social skills as well.
